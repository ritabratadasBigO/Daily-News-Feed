Image not found.IDFC FIRST Bank launches real time tracking for international money transfer through SWIFT system - ANI News

Published Date: Tue, 05 Nov 2024

IDFC FIRST Bank has launched real-time tracking for international money transfers, becoming the first bank in India to offer this service in association with Swift. The new feature, powered by the SWIFT Global Payments Innovation (GPI) platform, allows customers to track overseas payments through the bank's mobile app and internet banking platforms. This feature provides transparency and peace of mind, enabling customers to track the exact status of funds being transferred abroad. The bank's customers can now see if their funds are in transit, credited to the recipient bank, or if an issue has occurred. The service is particularly useful under the Reserve Bank of India's Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS), which allows resident individuals to send money abroad for various purposes. The bank offers this 24/7 tracking tool without any processing charges, setting a new standard for banking convenience in India.

Image not found.Aditya Birla Finance and Glas Trust seek removal of resolution professional in Byjus insolvency case - Storyboard18

Published Date: Tue, 05 Nov 2024

Aditya Birla Finance and Glas Trust Company have filed applications with the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) seeking the removal of the Resolution Professional handling the insolvency proceedings of Byju's parent company, Think and Learn. The companies claim that the RP's decision to classify only one creditor as a financial creditor has led to a skewed Committee of Creditors (CoC) composition. Senior advocates representing the companies argued that this has resulted in a single creditor holding 100% of the voting rights, and urged the NCLT to expedite the hearing. The matter has been scheduled for hearing next week. The NCLT has been instructed to resolve the dispute within two weeks. This development follows a recent Supreme Court ruling that allowed Glas Trust's appeal challenging a decision by the National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) that had stalled the insolvency proceedings.
