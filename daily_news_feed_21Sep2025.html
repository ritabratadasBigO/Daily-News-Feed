Image not found.PAYING RENT ON TIME - Cape Gazette

Published Date: Sat, 20 Sep 2025

Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac announced a policy change allowing mortgage lenders to use VantageScore credit ratings in addition to or instead of traditional FICO scores. This change expands credit access to individuals with limited credit history, particularly renters with a history of on-time rent payments. VantageScore considers rent payment history reported to major credit bureaus, while some FICO versions do not. The policy is expected to benefit first-time homebuyers and boost the housing market by increasing the pool of potential borrowers. This move also introduces competition in the credit score market, potentially leading to lower fees and closing costs for consumers. FICO, the creator of the FICO score, maintains that its score remains the industry standard and most reliable for evaluating creditworthiness.

Image not found.India cenbank to back sticking with headline inflation target ahead of review, sources say - WTVB

Published Date: Sat, 20 Sep 2025

India's central bank is poised to recommend retaining the existing 4% inflation target for a third consecutive term. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) received feedback from stakeholders, including economists and market participants, who largely supported the current framework. While minor adjustments to communication strategies may be considered, the core structure of flexible inflation targeting, with a 2% to 6% tolerance band, is expected to remain unchanged. The RBI's decision comes after a decade of largely successful implementation, with inflation staying within the target band for approximately three-quarters of the time. Despite calls from some stakeholders to shift focus to core inflation, the RBI is likely to maintain its focus on headline inflation, acknowledging the significant impact of food and fuel prices on overall inflation. The upcoming review of the consumer price index basket is expected to further reduce the weightage of food, potentially mitigating future volatility.

Image not found.Sensex, Nifty close higher as bank and IT stocks lead gains ahead of fed decision - DD News

Published Date: Sun, 21 Sep 2025

Indian equities closed higher on Wednesday, driven by gains in heavyweights like SBI, BEL, and Maruti Suzuki. The Sensex rose 313 points (1.2%) to 82,000, while the Nifty 50 advanced 150 points (1.1%) to 25,000. Investor sentiment remained cautiously optimistic despite ongoing trade discussions between India and the United States. Broader markets also saw positive momentum, with smallcap stocks experiencing value buying. Persistent foreign institutional investor (FII) selling and caution ahead of the U.S. Federal Reserve policy outcome capped gains.
