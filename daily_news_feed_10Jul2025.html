Image not found.The company behind your credit score is plunging on the stock market after a White House announcement - CNN

Published Date: Wed, 09 Jul 2025

The Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) announced that lenders can now use VantageScore, a competitor to FICO scores, to assess creditworthiness for government-backed mortgages. This move aims to increase competition in the credit scoring market and potentially lower closing costs for borrowers. The announcement comes amid reports of rising credit check fees from FICO, which has long dominated the mortgage market. FICO shares fell over 17% following the news, but analysts believe the impact on FICO's market dominance may be limited due to lenders' limited experience with VantageScore for mortgages. VantageScore, created by the three major credit bureaus, considers rent payments in its scoring model, a feature that FICO also now incorporates into its latest system. Both FICO and VantageScore aim to predict the likelihood of borrowers defaulting on debt repayments, with higher scores indicating lower risk.

Image not found.FICO reporting could stunt BNPL - Payments Dive

Published Date: Wed, 09 Jul 2025

FICO's decision to track and share buy now, pay later (BNPL) transaction data with credit bureaus could impact the BNPL industry's growth. While this move may improve credit scores for responsible BNPL users, it could also lead to BNPL providers extending credit less readily to consumers with multiple BNPL loans or a history of missed payments. Some industry experts believe this increased transparency could slow BNPL growth, as consumers may be less likely to utilize the service if their financial behavior is closely monitored. However, others argue that BNPL's widespread adoption and consumer demand for credit will continue to drive growth, regardless of credit reporting. Ultimately, the long-term impact of FICO's decision on the BNPL industry remains to be seen, as it will likely take several years for BNPL data to significantly influence credit scores. Despite the potential for change, BNPL continues to be a popular payment option, with global spending projected to reach $450 billion by 2027.
