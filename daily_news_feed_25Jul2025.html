Image not found.FICO Survey: 7 in 10 APAC Bank Leaders Say Scams and Mule Accounts Are Now the Dominant Fraud Concern - CXOToday.com

Published Date: Thu, 24 Jul 2025

A recent FICO poll reveals that scams and mule accounts are the primary threat facing banks in the Asia Pacific region, with 69% of senior banking executives citing them as their biggest concern. These scams, often facilitated through social media and messaging apps, are particularly challenging as they involve authorized payments, making them difficult to detect and recover from. The poll highlights the severity of the issue, with scam-related losses reaching record highs in countries like Singapore, Thailand, and Malaysia. Banks face internal challenges in combating these scams, with siloed data and a lack of integrated systems hindering their ability to effectively respond. While there are differing opinions on bank responsibility for reimbursing victims, the poll underscores the urgent need for financial institutions to strengthen their defenses against evolving scam tactics. FICO emphasizes the importance of connected systems and real-time insights for banks to stay ahead of these rapidly changing threats.

Image not found.Indian Bank expects its new UPI app to aid cost saving - BusinessLine

Published Date: Thu, 24 Jul 2025

Indian Bank has launched its own UPI app, INDUPI, after spending significant sums on third-party UPI transaction fees. The app, currently in a closed beta phase, allows customers to conduct UPI transactions within the Indian Bank ecosystem. The bank aims to expand INDUPI to all customers pending approval from the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI). Indian Bank's MD & CEO, Binod Kumar, stated that developing the app was cost-effective and will enable the bank to offer benefits like cashbacks and scratch cards to customers using INDUPI. The bank plans to integrate INDUPI into its existing mobile banking app for enhanced user experience. This move positions Indian Bank alongside other banks, both public and private, who have developed their own UPI solutions.

Image not found.EIB partners with Rabobank in €1bn SME financing deal - Impact Investor

Published Date: Thu, 24 Jul 2025

The European Investment Bank (EIB) has committed €500 million to Rabobank and its subsidiary DLL to support European businesses in climate-relevant and agricultural projects. Both institutions will match the EIB's funding, resulting in a total of €1 billion available for investment. Rabobank will focus on Dutch small-scale sustainability and agriculture projects, while DLL will target SMEs and midcaps across nine European countries for local sustainability investments. DLL believes the funding will enhance its ability to provide accessible and affordable leasing solutions for sustainable equipment and technology.
