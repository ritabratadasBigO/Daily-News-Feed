Published Date: Tue, 08 Jul 2025

Fair Isaac Corporation (FICO), the long-dominant player in credit scoring, faces increasing pressure from competitors and regulatory changes. The Federal Housing Finance Agency's (FHFA) decision to allow VantageScore for government-backed mortgages has challenged FICO's market share, particularly in the lucrative mortgage sector. VantageScore's advantages include inclusivity, incorporating alternative data like rent payments, and offering more comprehensive risk assessment tools. FICO's reliance on high fees and legacy systems is increasingly seen as outdated in a digitized world. Investors are concerned about FICO's future profitability as its core lending fees are projected to remain flat. Credit bureau stocks, such as Equifax and TransUnion, which own VantageScore, are seen as better positioned to benefit from the evolving credit scoring landscape. FICO shareholders may need to brace for underperformance unless the company adapts to the changing market dynamics by embracing AI and alternative data.

Image not found.Achmea discontinues pension admin business - Investment & Pensions Europe

Published Date: Tue, 08 Jul 2025

Achmea Pensioenservices (APS), a Dutch pension administrator, will cease servicing external pension funds by January 1, 2028. This decision stems from APS's lack of scale to compete effectively, exacerbated by the departure of its largest client, Rabobank Pensioenfonds. APS has been chronically lossmaking, with operational losses doubling to €63 million last year. The firm will focus on transitioning its remaining clients to defined contribution (DC) arrangements and guiding them to new providers. APS employs approximately 350 people, and while some jobs will be lost, the company will continue to provide administration services for its own pension schemes. Achmea, APS's parent company, has allocated €175 million to wind down the pension administration operations.
