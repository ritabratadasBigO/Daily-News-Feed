Image not found.India Insurtech Summit to explore regulatory shifts and digital push in $11 trillion insurance market - MediaBrief

Published Date: Tue, 08 Jul 2025

The Indian insurance sector is undergoing a significant digital transformation, driven by initiatives from the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI). IRDAI's "Insurance for All by 2047" mission aims to deepen penetration through digital marketplaces like Bima Sugam, relaxed product filing norms, and microinsurance policies. The regulator is also introducing guidelines to encourage digitization throughout the policy lifecycle, from onboarding to claims settlement. A second phase of the regulatory sandbox will support innovation in areas like usage-based insurance and embedded products. Digital transformation is expected to lower acquisition costs, improve customer experience, and open new market opportunities. IRDAI is also exploring a composite insurance license, allowing companies to offer both life and general insurance, potentially leading to consolidation and enhanced customer coverage.

Image not found.These Banks Have Removed Minimum Balance Requirement: Check List - NDTV

Published Date: Tue, 08 Jul 2025

Several major Indian banks have eliminated compulsory Average Monthly Balance (AMB) requirements for most savings accounts, aiming to improve customer experience and financial inclusion. Bank of Baroda, Punjab National Bank, Indian Bank, and Canara Bank have all implemented this change, effective from July 2025 onwards. These banks will no longer impose penalties on customers who fail to maintain the required AMB. State Bank of India, the first to adopt this policy in 2020, reported positive impacts on first-time account holders. The move is seen as a response to evolving market dynamics and a commitment to providing greater value to customers. This shift is expected to benefit various segments, particularly women, farmers, and low-income households, by reducing financial barriers to banking services.
