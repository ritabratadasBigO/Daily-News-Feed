The Supreme Court of India recently ruled in the case of PNB Housing Finance Limited versus Sh. Manoj Saha, clarifying the conflict between the SARFAESI Act and state rent control laws regarding tenant rights in secured assets. The court overturned a High Court order that had restored possession of a secured asset to a tenant, finding insufficient evidence to prove a pre-existing tenancy. The Supreme Court emphasized the importance of tangible evidence, such as rent receipts and utility bills, to support tenancy claims. It also highlighted the tenant's responsibility to act promptly and inform the secured creditor of their rights under the SARFAESI Act. The decision underscores the need for clear documentation and timely action when asserting tenancy rights in cases involving secured assets and the SARFAESI Act. The court's ruling sets a precedent for future cases involving similar disputes, emphasizing the need for robust evidence to support tenancy claims.
