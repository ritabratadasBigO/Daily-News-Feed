Image not found.Gen Z leads biggest drop in FICO scores since financial crisis - The Edge Malaysia

Published Date: Wed, 17 Sep 2025

US consumer credit quality experienced its worst decline since the 2008 financial crisis, with the average FICO score dropping to 715 in April 2023 from 717 in the previous year. Gen Z borrowers were most affected, witnessing a three-point decline in their average FICO score to 676, marking the largest drop among all age groups since 2020. This decline is attributed to increased credit utilization and delinquency rates, including the resumption of reporting student loan delinquencies, which reached a record high. Gen Z borrowers are disproportionately burdened by student loan debt, with 34% still making payments compared to 17% of the overall population. While the average FICO score fell, the median score continued to rise, indicating that a small number of significant score drops influenced the overall average. Credit card utilization also increased, reaching 30% after hitting a low of 15% in April 2021.

Image not found.Cash Flow Data Lets Banks See Borrowers That FICO Scores Miss - PYMNTS.com

Published Date: Thu, 18 Sep 2025

Consumer perception of credit availability often lags reality, with many believing they would be denied credit even when they are likely to be approved. This "psychological access gap" is exacerbated by traditional underwriting methods that rely heavily on static credit scores, overlooking real-time income and spending data. To address this, banks and FinTechs are increasingly adopting cash flow underwriting, which analyzes an applicant's actual income and spending patterns to determine their repayment capacity. This approach can expand credit access to individuals with limited credit history or strong cash management habits. Major financial institutions like Chase and PayPal are partnering with companies like Nova Credit to implement cash flow underwriting, while neobanks like Dave are leveraging AI-driven engines for this purpose. While promising, cash flow underwriting requires robust data security and consumer consent to ensure responsible lending practices and avoid algorithmic bias.

Image not found.Ripple Announces Major Partnership with $1.5 Trillion Financial Giant Franklin Templeton and DBS Bank Singapore - TradingView

Published Date: Thu, 18 Sep 2025

Ripple has partnered with Franklin Templeton and DBS Bank to develop blockchain-powered repo markets using stablecoins and tokenized collateral. This initiative will see Franklin Templeton's sgBENJI token, representing shares in a tokenized U.S. dollar short-term money market fund, listed on the Singaporean exchange and exchangeable for Ripple's RLUSD stablecoin. The partnership aims to provide investors with a seamless transition to a safer, yield-bearing product during market volatility. The next phase will involve using sgBENJI tokens as collateral to secure short-term loans from DBS or other lenders, mirroring traditional repo market operations. Franklin Templeton will issue sgBENJI tokens on the XRP Ledger and other blockchains, with RLUSD serving as the base currency for trading. Ripple believes this integration of tokenized money market funds and stablecoins will revolutionize repo markets.
