Image not found.Bank Account Attached by Income Tax Dept: Madras HC Allows Instalment Payment of 20% Tax, Orders De-Freezing After 1st instalment [Read Order] - Taxscan

Published Date: Tue, 15 Jul 2025

The Madras High Court has partially lifted an Income Tax attachment order on Ms Devadharshan Food Products, a fruit pulp manufacturer. The court ruled that the company must pay 20% of the disputed tax demand, exceeding ₹1 crore, in five equal monthly installments of ₹6 lakh, starting July 2025. This payment schedule allows the company's bank accounts, previously frozen by the Income Tax Department, to be defreezed upon payment of the first installment. The court acknowledged the seasonal nature of the company's business and its willingness to make partial payments. However, the court warned that any missed installment would automatically revive the attachment order. The company had challenged the attachment notices issued under Section 226(3) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, arguing that the premature freezing of its accounts significantly impacted its operations.

Image not found.India's Bajaj Finserv AM finds best risk-reward in 30-year bonds, fund manager says - TradingView

Published Date: Tue, 15 Jul 2025

Bajaj Finserv Asset Management's senior fund manager for fixed income, Siddharth Chaudhary, believes India's 30-year government bond currently offers the most compelling risk-reward profile. This view stems from the bond absorbing much of the recent market pressure, despite ultralong bonds surrendering earlier gains following the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) policy shift in June. Chaudhary anticipates the 10-year benchmark bond yield to fall to the 6.50%-7.00% range, assuming persistent market expectations of a rate cut. While acknowledging the RBI's potential dovish stance, Chaudhary doesn't foresee a rate cut in the August policy meeting, emphasizing the need to assess rate transmission and growth data. He suggests the RBI will use the August meeting to signal its intentions effectively after the abrupt policy change in June. Chaudhary believes the chances of a 25 basis point rate cut later this year have improved due to inflation trending below projections and subdued growth.
