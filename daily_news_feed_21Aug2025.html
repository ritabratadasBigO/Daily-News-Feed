DBS Bank India Limited has launched DBS Treasures, a premium banking and wealth management program tailored for high-net-worth individuals. The program offers personalized banking, wealth management, lifestyle, travel, and health privileges based on a tiered system based on Total Relationship Value (TRV). TRV is calculated by aggregating average quarterly balances across various accounts, including savings, term deposits, insurance, investments, and mortgages. DBS Treasures provides exclusive benefits such as signature lifestyle privileges, exclusive offers, unlimited ATM withdrawals, and a low forex markup on international transactions. The program also includes access to DBS Study Abroad Total Assist and DBS Golden Circle, offering support for education abroad and exclusive travel benefits. To qualify for DBS Treasures, individuals need to maintain a minimum TRV of INR 30 Lacs.

Image not found.NCLT approves Rs 2,072-cr resolution plan for Hindustan National Glass - Insolvency Tracker

Published Date: Thu, 21 Aug 2025

The National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) has approved a ₹2,000 crore resolution plan for Hindustan National Glass & Industries Limited (HNGIL), ending its insolvency proceedings initiated by DBS Bank Limited in 2021. The plan, submitted by Independent Sugar Corporation Limited (INSCO), received overwhelming support from the Committee of Creditors (CoC) and will be distributed among creditors, with a significant portion allocated to financial creditors. This approval follows the Supreme Court's rejection of an earlier plan by AGI Greenpac Limited due to lack of Competition Commission of India (CCI) approval. HNGIL, a leading Indian container glass manufacturer, had an admitted financial debt of ₹3,000 crores at the time of insolvency. INSCO, a Ugandan multinational, is entering the Indian manufacturing sector through this acquisition, marking its first foray into the container glass market. The CCI approved the acquisition under the green channel as it did not pose a competitive threat.
