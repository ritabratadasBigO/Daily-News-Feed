Cardinal Financial Completes Issue of First Ever Mortgage-Backed Security Containing Loans Decisioned with the help of FICO Score 10 T - Business Wire

Published Date: Tue, 03 Dec 2024

FICO, a global analytics software leader, has announced a significant milestone in housing finance with the launch of the first government-issued mortgage-backed security (MBS) using FICO Score 10 T. This historic MBS pool, issued by Cardinal Financial, includes VA loans assessed through FICO's most innovative scoring model, providing better predictive power for lenders and improving loan options for borrowers. FICO Score 10 T incorporates trended data to provide a more comprehensive understanding of credit behavior over time, enabling more accurate risk assessments and expanding opportunities for borrowers, including veterans and active-duty military personnel. Cardinal Financial was one of the first lenders to adopt FICO Score 10 T and has reported that the majority of borrowers received higher credit scores, allowing for more favorable loan terms with no observable increase in credit risk. The issuance is expected to enhance liquidity for future loans decisioned with FICO Score 10 T and further expand the use of FICO's newest credit score in the secondary market. FICO is committed to assisting mortgage industry participants in transitioning to its most predictive scoring model, providing a detailed guide and best practices for a smooth transition.

Regulatory shifts and market trends: Insights from Spain - Global Trade Review (GTR)

Published Date: Tue, 03 Dec 2024

A Madrid roundtable hosted by WTW brought together leading banks and insurers to discuss key trends shaping the financial landscape in 2024 and beyond. Participants highlighted regulatory uncertainty surrounding Basel implementation, a continued focus on developed markets leaving a trade finance gap, and the increasing importance of ESG factors. Discussions centered on adapting to a dynamic environment, developing new products and solutions, particularly in credit insurance, and leveraging technology to improve risk management. The panel also addressed the significant trade finance gap, particularly in emerging markets like Africa, emphasizing the need for better risk assessment and collaboration between banks and insurers. Technology's role in streamlining processes, improving data sharing, and enhancing efficiency was a key theme, with a focus on platform interconnectivity and AI's potential in credit analysis. Looking ahead, participants expressed optimism for future growth, but also emphasized the need for increased capacity in emerging markets, better data sharing, and stronger partnerships between banks, insurers, and brokers.
