Image not found.Why Fair Isaac Corporation Fell This Week - Mitrade

Published Date: Fri, 11 Jul 2025

Fair Isaac Corporation (FICO), the creator of the widely used FICO credit score, is facing challenges from a new competitor, VantageScore, backed by the Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA). FHFA Director Bill Pulte announced that Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac will allow lenders to use VantageScore, potentially impacting FICO's market dominance. This news has led to a decline in FICO's stock price as the GSEs guarantee a significant portion of U.S. mortgages. While VantageScore is currently an option for a smaller subset of borrowers, its wider adoption could limit FICO's ability to raise prices aggressively. The FHFA's move is seen as an effort to lower costs for homeowners and potentially prevent the adoption of a "bimerge" scoring system, which would further reduce FICO's market share. Despite the challenges, investors remain optimistic about FICO's long-term prospects, as its stock still trades at a high valuation.
