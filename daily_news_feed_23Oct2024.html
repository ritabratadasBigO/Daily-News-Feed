FICO and U.S. Senator Mark Warner Continue Partnership to Support Financial Literacy for Virginia Residents - sharewise

Published Date: Tue, 22 Oct 2024

FICO, a global analytics software leader, and U.S. Senator Mark Warner's office are partnering to host a series of credit education workshops in Virginia. The workshops, part of the Virginia Financial Empowerment Series, aim to enhance financial literacy throughout the state. The first workshop on October 29 is open to individuals and small business owners, focusing on improving and maintaining a strong FICO Score for personal financial health and business growth. The second workshop on October 30 is designed specifically for small business owners, providing insights on how credit can facilitate access to capital and support longterm business success. FICO's Score A Better Future program is a free credit education initiative that offers resources on how FICO Scores influence lending decisions and provides actionable steps for improving financial health. The workshops are free and open to the public, and attendees will have the opportunity to connect with Money Management International for free credit counseling and guidance in setting financial goals.

FCA fines VWFS 5.4m for failing to support customers in financial hardship - Motor Finance

Published Date: Tue, 22 Oct 2024

Volkswagen Financial Services (UK) Limited has been fined 10 million by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) for failing to provide tailored support to customers in financial difficulty. The company also agreed to pay over 1 million in compensation to approximately 110,000 customers who may have been affected by its shortcomings. The FCA investigation found that VWFS failed to adequately understand its customers' individual financial situations, resulting in a lack of tailored support. In some cases, vulnerable customers lost their cars, worsening their financial and personal circumstances. The FCA has also created a redress scheme for affected customers and has taken steps to improve VWFS's staff training, communications, and debt collection model. The fine and redress scheme signal the importance of properly supporting customers in financial difficulty, according to the FCA.
