Image not found.What Effects Would Mass Student Loan Defaults Have on the US Economy? - Investopedia

Published Date: Mon, 14 Jul 2025

Nearly 25% of the $1.7 trillion federal student loan portfolio is at risk of default, with 10 million borrowers over 90 days delinquent as of April 2023. The U.S. Department of Education resumed collections on defaulted loans in May 2023, potentially leading to wage garnishment and impacting borrowers' credit scores. While the delinquency rate appears to be plateauing, mass defaults could negatively impact the economy through reduced consumer spending and potential job losses. The federal government may see a short-term revenue boost from collected payments, but this could be offset by long-term economic consequences. Policies favoring debt collection over forgiveness may hinder borrowers' financial mobility and economic advancement. The situation highlights the need for comprehensive solutions that address both borrower needs and the government's financial interests.
