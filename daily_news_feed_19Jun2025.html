Image not found.Farm organisations question Rabobank's dairy projections - Irish Farmers journal

Published Date: Wed, 18 Jun 2025

Rabobank predicts a natural correction in dairy markets, anticipating a slowdown in the second half of the year due to expanding supply and demand uncertainty. However, Irish farm organizations challenge this analysis, citing positive supply-demand dynamics and strong market projections for next year. They argue that predicting milk prices beyond three months is unreliable due to factors like weather, animal health, and geopolitical instability. Rabobank attributes the anticipated slowdown to waning consumer sentiment amid weak global economic conditions and challenges in passing on higher dairy costs to consumers. The bank also highlights ongoing trade tensions and tariff volatility as key risks to the dairy trade. Despite these concerns, RaboResearch forecasts a significant increase in global milk production in 2025, driven by growth in major exporting regions.

Image not found.Planning for your Institution’s financial sustainability - Davy

Published Date: Wed, 18 Jun 2025

This article emphasizes the importance of financial sustainability for institutional entities like charities and non-profits. It argues that sustainable investing, aligning investment strategies with an institution's values and mission, is crucial for long-term viability. A key tool for achieving financial sustainability is the Investment Policy Statement (IPS), which outlines the institution's investment objectives, strategies, and fiduciary responsibilities. The IPS should be tailored to the specific needs and risk tolerance of each institution and reviewed annually to ensure alignment with evolving goals. Strong governance is essential throughout the IPS planning, implementation, and oversight process, with trustees and investment committees clearly articulating their investment approach and its support for the institution's mission. The article encourages institutions to begin planning for their financial sustainability by developing a comprehensive IPS and engaging in ongoing evaluation and adaptation.
