Image not found.Ag keeps sunny side up in confidence survey - farmersweekly.co.nz

Published Date: Mon, 15 Sep 2025

New Zealand farmer confidence reached its second-highest level in a decade, driven by record milk prices and strong revenue from sheepmeat and beef. The Rabobank Rural Confidence Survey revealed a net reading of 46%, marking the fourth consecutive quarter of elevated sentiment. Farmers are optimistic about the broader agricultural economy, with 51% expecting improvement in the coming year, primarily due to rising commodity prices. Dairy and sheep & beef farmers are particularly optimistic, while horticulturalists are less so due to less dramatic price increases. Investment intentions remain largely unchanged, with dairy farmers showing the strongest inclination to invest. While farmers expressed some concern about the recent increase in US tariffs on New Zealand goods, the average level of worry was moderate.
