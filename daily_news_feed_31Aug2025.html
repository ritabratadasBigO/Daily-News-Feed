Image not found.India’s Q1 GDP stuns, but here are 5 startling contradictions you cannot ignore - financialexpress.com

Published Date: Sun, 31 Aug 2025

India's Q1 GDP growth surprised analysts, exceeding expectations at a robust %. However, IDFC First Bank cautions that this growth was partly driven by transient factors like a favorable base effect and frontloaded exports, which may wane in the second half of the fiscal year. The bank points to subdued core inflation and a low current account deficit as indicators of a potential output gap, suggesting that the economy is not operating at full potential. While rural consumption appears to be recovering, urban consumption indicators remain weak, likely due to slowing real wage growth in urban areas. IDFC First Bank anticipates a slowdown in GDP growth in H2FY26 due to factors like a pickup in inflation and the impact of US tariffs on Indian exports. Despite the strong Q1 performance, the bank maintains its forecast for a 25 bps rate cut by the RBI in December, citing the need for further clarity on economic trends and the impact of fiscal measures.

Image not found.Australia keeps pumping house prices, while Trump pursues 'state-run capitalism' - Australian Broadcasting Corporation

Published Date: Sat, 30 Aug 2025

Rabobank strategist Ben Picton argues that the US and Australian economies are undergoing significant shifts, challenging traditional economic paradigms. He criticizes the politicization of the Federal Reserve under President Trump and questions the sustainability of Australia's housing wealth, driven largely by real estate. Picton expresses concern over the Australian government's expanded Home Guarantee Scheme, which allows first-time buyers with 5% deposits, potentially exacerbating housing market speculation. He contrasts this with the US government's increasing intervention in strategic industries like semiconductor manufacturing, highlighting a shift towards dirigiste economic policies. Picton warns that markets pricing for a continuation of globalization may be vulnerable to disruptions caused by these evolving economic landscapes. The US Court of Appeals recently ruled against most of Trump's tariffs, further adding to the uncertainty and volatility in the global market.
