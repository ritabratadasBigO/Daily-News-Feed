Image not found.NBFCs raise funds via ECB route as banks tighten loan pipeline - BusinessLine

Published Date: Sun, 08 Dec 2024

Top-rated private and government-owned non-banking finance companies (NBFCs) are tapping the external commercial borrowing (ECB) market to raise funds due to banks' reluctance to lend. PNB Housing Finance plans to raise $100-125 million each in two tranches by FY25 end, while Shriram Finance aims to raise up to $1 billion. Power Finance Corp and REC have proposed raising $200 million and $500 million, respectively, via ECBs. Private NBFCs such as HDB Financial and Cholamandalam Finance have proposed raising $250 million each via ECBs. Highly rated NBFCs diversify their funding sources to balance pricing and investor demands, while low-rated NBFCs face challenges in accessing domestic bank loans and bonds. The RBI has asked top-rated NBFCs to maintain at least 25% of their overall liabilities via the capital market route, and CRISIL Ratings expects alternate funding sources such as securitization and bonds to rise within the overall resource mix of NBFCs.

Insurance Claim Cannot Be Denied For Unrelated Health Issues: Chandigarh State Commission Holds Bajaj... - Live Law - Indian Legal News

Published Date: Sun, 08 Dec 2024

The State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, Chandigarh, held Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance Company liable for rejecting an insurance policy due to non-disclosure of pre-existing medical conditions. The complainant's mother had obtained a personal loan and a group term life insurance policy, which was valid from 2020 to 2021. The policy was rejected by the insurance company, citing nondisclosure of diabetes and hypertension. The commission observed that the loan was taken after careful consideration, and there was no documentary evidence to prove the deceased was suffering from such diseases at the time of enrollment. The commission dismissed the appeal and upheld the decision of the district commission, directing the insurance company to pay Rs.3,50,000 with interest and compensation. The case highlights the importance of investigating the cause of death and the burden of proof on the insurer to prove the basis for repudiation of a claim.

Invest in Ambani, Adani stocks with Aditya Birla SL Conglomerate Fund NFO. 10 things to know - The Economic Times

Published Date: Sun, 08 Dec 2024

Aditya Birla Sun Life Mutual Fund has launched a new fund, the Aditya Birla Sun Life Conglomerate Fund, which allows investors to diversify their portfolio by investing in top Indian business conglomerates. The fund aims to provide long-term capital appreciation by investing in equity and equity-related securities of companies that follow conglomerate themes. The fund will track the BSE Select Business Groups Index TRI and will be managed by Kunal Sangoi and Harish Krishnan. The minimum application amount is Rs 100, and the scheme will allocate 80-100% in equity and equity-related instruments of companies that are part of the conglomerate theme. The fund includes a mix of large, mid, and small-cap stocks, offering a balanced risk-reward profile. The fund managers use a bottom-up approach to identify high-growth opportunities within these conglomerates, providing investors with a unique opportunity to harness their growth potential.
