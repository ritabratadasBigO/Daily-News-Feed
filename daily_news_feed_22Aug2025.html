Image not found.NCLT Allows Liquidation of Reliance Capital ltd, Appoints Liquidator [Read Order] - Taxscan

Published Date: Thu, 21 Aug 2025

The Mumbai bench of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) has approved the liquidation of Reliance Capital Ltd. under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016. Mr. Pankaj Sham Joshi, the applicant, filed a petition seeking liquidation after the Corporate Debtor, Win and Trendz Exim Private Limited, failed to cooperate in the Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP). Despite efforts to invite expressions of interest from potential resolution applicants, none were received due to a lack of information about the Corporate Debtor's assets and financial status. The Committee of Creditors (COC) nominated Mr. Anil Seetaram Vaidya as the liquidator, who has been appointed by the NCLT. The Corporate Debtor will now be liquidated in accordance with the provisions of the IBC and applicable regulations. This decision marks the end of the CIRP process for Reliance Capital Ltd.

Image not found.DBS Bank Deepens Crypto Push with Ethereum-Based Structured Notes - CoinLaw

Published Date: Thu, 21 Aug 2025

DBS Bank, Singapore's largest bank, has launched the first tokenized structured notes on the Ethereum blockchain, expanding access to crypto investment products for institutional and accredited investors. These notes, distributed through platforms like ADDX, DigiFT, and HydraX, offer cash payouts linked to cryptocurrency price increases while incorporating mechanisms to limit potential losses. This move builds on Singapore's Project Guardian, a regulatory framework promoting the tokenization of real-world assets, and marks DBS's first foray onto a public blockchain. The bank aims to cater to the growing demand from family offices and wealth managers seeking alternative investments, particularly those tied to digital assets. The tokenized notes, with a minimum investment of $1,000, offer greater accessibility and portfolio flexibility compared to traditional structured notes, which often required minimum investments of $100,000. DBS plans to tokenize other financial instruments, including those linked to equities and credit markets, as demand for tokenized investments continues to rise.
