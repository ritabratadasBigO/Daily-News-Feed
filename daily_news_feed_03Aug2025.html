Image not found.Debt Arising From Structured Supply Chain Finance Facility Does Not Constitute 'Financial Debt' U/S 5(8)... - Live Law

Published Date: Sat, 02 Aug 2025

The National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) in New Delhi ruled that debt arising from a structured supply chain finance facility is not considered financial debt under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016. The case involved Prudent Arc Limited seeking insolvency proceedings against Karan Automobiles for a default of Rs. Cr. The NCLT determined that the transaction's nature was operational, as funds were advanced to facilitate the supply of goods and services, not for the time value of money. The tribunal emphasized that the primary obligation to pay remained with the buyer (HEMA Engineering), and the corporate debtor's fallback liability did not change the arrangement's character. This decision aligns with previous rulings that classify invoice discounting transactions as operational debt unless disbursed for the time value of money. The application for insolvency proceedings was subsequently dismissed.
