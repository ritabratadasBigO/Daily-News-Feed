Image not found.First-hand farming insights from across the globe thanks to Rabobank Knowledge Tours - Queensland Country Life

Rabobank is offering agricultural knowledge tours, providing Australian farmers with insights into global farming practices. Greg and Leanne Evans, Chinchilla cotton growers, will participate in the Brazil Cotton Tour, joining other Australian producers to learn from Brazilian farmers. The Evans family has been growing dryland cotton for 35 years and believes cotton is a profitable and versatile crop, particularly in their region. They value learning from other farmers, both through Rabobank's tours and by contracting cotton picking in NSW's Macquarie Valley. Rabobank's All in One account simplifies their financial management, allowing them to easily track their finances. The Evans family is excited about the future of their cotton operation, with their children now involved in the harvest.

Image not found.Live: Australian stocks pull back from record highs - Australian Broadcasting Corporation

Published Date: Sun, 20 Jul 2025

The Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) opened sharply lower on Monday, with the ASX 200 falling over 1% to 8,674 points. This decline follows a record close on Friday and is attributed to profit-taking ahead of the August earnings season. The financial sector is leading the decline, with heavy losses in major banks. Other interest-rate sensitive sectors like consumer discretionary and real estate are also under pressure. Despite the overall market downturn, the materials and energy sectors are showing resilience. Block, the owner of Afterpay, bucked the trend, surging over 5% after being added to the S&P 500 index. Coking coal prices have dropped to their lowest level since April, driven by easing supply concerns and reduced demand from China. Insignia Financial shares fell sharply after the company confirmed ongoing discussions with CC Capital for a potential buyout, but there is no guarantee of a deal. Meanwhile, the Reserve Bank of Australia's decision to hold interest rates steady last week has surprised markets, with economists now predicting a rate cut in August. PSG, fresh off their Champions League victory, is seeking to bolster their squad under manager Luis Enrique.
