Image not found.DBS Bank India Waives Foreclosure Charges for MSME Borrowers - SME Street

Published Date: Sat, 19 Jul 2025

DBS Bank India has announced the waiver of prepayment and foreclosure charges for all Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSME) borrowers registered with UDYAM. This move aims to enhance borrower flexibility and aligns with regulatory guidance promoting transparency in lending. The waiver applies to both new and existing active borrowers, effective immediately. DBS Bank India believes this change will enable businesses to optimize cash flow management by repaying loans early without penalty. The bank emphasizes its commitment to supporting MSMEs with need-based solutions that strengthen their resilience and foster long-term growth. DBS Bank India offers a comprehensive suite of SME banking solutions, including online account opening, business loans, foreign exchange services, and digital banking platforms.

Image not found.How Nepali students studying abroad can take loans: Everything you need to know - The Kirib

Published Date: Sun, 20 Jul 2025

Nepali students pursuing higher education abroad often rely on education loans to cover significant expenses. Before leaving Nepal, students can apply for loans from commercial banks, development banks, or finance companies, typically requiring collateral from the student's family. Loan limits vary depending on the lender and collateral value, with repayment starting after course completion and extending up to seven years. Accessing loans while abroad is more challenging, with most international lenders requiring cosigners. Some platforms offer international student loans without cosigners but often at higher interest rates. Nepali banks may issue loans to students abroad if a guardian in Nepal assumes responsibility, but pre-departure planning is crucial due to the requirement for physical signatures.
