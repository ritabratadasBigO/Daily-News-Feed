Image not found.Slovakia preparing to drop veto on EU's Russia sanctions - EUobserver

Published Date: Mon, 14 Jul 2025

Slovakia's Prime Minister Robert Fico indicated that he will lift his veto on the 18th round of Russia sanctions if the EU provides "political guarantees" regarding potential financial repercussions for Slovakia after a 2028 cutoff of Russian gas purchases. Fico has been blocking the sanctions due to concerns over billions of euros in potential costs to Slovakia. He seeks assurances from EU member states, particularly Germany and Poland, regarding compensation for higher gas transit fees and potential lawsuits from Russia. While Fico met with the EU Commission and spoke with German Chancellor Friedrich Merz and Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk, no concrete legal guarantees were offered. Fico acknowledged the pressure to align with the EU stance but emphasized his desire to protect Slovakia's interests. He faces criticism for his stance, which some view as prioritizing domestic political gain over EU solidarity.
