Published Date: Sat, 06 Sep 2025

Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac announced a policy change allowing mortgage lenders to use VantageScore credit ratings alongside or instead of traditional FICO scores. This change expands credit access for borrowers with limited credit history, particularly renters with a history of on-time rent payments. VantageScore considers rent payment history, which is not always factored into FICO scores, potentially benefiting first-time homebuyers. The move is expected to increase competition in the credit score market, potentially leading to lower fees and closing costs for consumers. FICO, the creator of the FICO score, maintains that its score is the industry standard and most predictive of creditworthiness. Despite the competition, Fannie and Freddie's decision is anticipated to boost the housing market by expanding the pool of eligible borrowers.
