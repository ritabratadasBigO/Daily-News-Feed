Image not found.Klarna and Afterpay Opt Not to Send BNPL Data to Credit Bureaus - PaymentsJournal

Published Date: Wed, 06 Aug 2025

Klarna and Afterpay have declined to participate in a new credit scoring model that incorporates buy now, pay later (BNPL) loan information, unlike competitor Affirm. Affirm has been working with FICO to develop credit score models including BNPL data, and has begun reporting its loan data to credit bureaus. Klarna and Afterpay express concerns that credit bureaus don't receive real-time, accurate BNPL data, which could negatively impact consumers' creditworthiness. They argue that BNPL products are designed to be accessible credit options and that current scoring models are not equipped to handle BNPL data. Despite these objections, FICO data suggests that including BNPL data has minimal impact on credit scores. Affirm refutes the notion of "phantom debt" associated with BNPL, stating that delinquencies are rare and BNPL loans represent a small fraction of credit card debt.
