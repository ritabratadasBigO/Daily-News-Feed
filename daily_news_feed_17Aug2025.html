Image not found.What is the minimum balance required in SBI, PNB, ICICI, HDFC savings account? - Times Bull

Published Date: Sat, 16 Aug 2025

ICICI Bank recently revised its minimum average balance (MAB) policy for new accounts, reducing it to Rs 15,000 for urban and metro areas, Rs 7,500 for semi-urban areas, and Rs 2,500 for rural branches. This change follows initial backlash against the bank's previous MAB requirement of Rs 50,000 for metro and urban areas. While government banks like SBI, PNB, and Canara Bank have eliminated MAB requirements for most accounts, private sector banks like ICICI, HDFC, and Axis continue to impose them. SBI, PNB, and Canara Bank have removed MAB requirements for all rural and metro savings accounts, offering zero-balance account operation. Other banks have varying MAB policies, with Union Bank requiring Rs 1,000 (with cheque book) or Rs 500 (without cheque book) in urban areas. HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Axis Bank have MAB requirements ranging from Rs 10,000 to Rs 15,000 for urban areas. Customers are advised to carefully review their bank's MAB policy to avoid potential penalties.

Image not found.India's RBI Approves Blockchain MSME Financing Platform to Boost Credit Access - AInvest

Published Date: Sat, 16 Aug 2025

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has approved IBDIC's blockchain platform, marking the first successful exit from the RBI's Regulatory Sandbox. This platform streamlines MSME financing by tokenizing invoices from large corporations, enabling faster and more secure credit access for smaller suppliers. Major banks like ICICI and HDFC support the system, enhancing adoption and reducing credit risks through shared ledgers. The initiative addresses India's significant MSME funding gap, estimated at over Rs 2025 lakh crore, by leveraging blockchain technology for greater transparency and digital finance integration. This approval signifies the RBI's growing confidence in blockchain for enterprise finance and could pave the way for similar solutions in other sectors. The RBI's evolving sandbox model, transitioning to an "OnTap" system, further accelerates fintech innovation by allowing for continuous testing and adaptation.

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has approved a blockchain-based platform developed by the Indian Banks' Digital Infrastructure Company (IBDIC) to revolutionize financing for MSMEs. This marks the first blockchain project to exit the RBI's Regulatory Sandbox, signifying a strong endorsement of blockchain technology in mainstream Indian finance. The platform enables the conversion of corporate supply chain invoices into digital tokens on a secure blockchain, facilitating instant, verified credit for MSMEs. This innovation reduces fraud risks, improves working capital efficiency, and expands lender participation by providing trust and transparency in financial transactions. Key supporters include major Indian banks, further solidifying the system's credibility and paving the way for industry-wide adoption. This move addresses India's significant MSME credit gap and positions the country as a leader in leveraging blockchain technology for inclusive financial growth.
