Image not found.FICO collaborates with HBCUs to combat data bias - AFRO American Newspapers

Published Date: Sun, 27 Jul 2025

FICO, a leading credit reporting agency, is partnering with historically Black colleges and universities (HBCUs) to address data bias in AI-driven financial models. The partnership, through the HBCU Educational Analytics Challenge, trains students in interpreting and refining AI datasets, empowering them to mitigate negative impacts on Black communities in areas like credit scoring and fraud detection. A 2021 Stanford report highlighted "noisy data" in AI models, which can disproportionately disadvantage Black and low-wealth communities. FICO's initiative aims to diversify the field of data analytics and promote responsible AI practices by fostering inclusion and addressing biases. Students participating in the challenge gain practical experience in analyzing data and understanding its implications for financial decisions, recognizing the importance of diverse perspectives in shaping these models. FICO's efforts demonstrate a commitment to creating fairer and more equitable financial systems by empowering the next generation of data professionals from underrepresented communities.

Image not found.Fuel prices to rise slightly despite global oil decline - Taipei Times

Published Date: Sun, 27 Jul 2025

Despite a decline in international crude oil prices, gasoline and diesel prices in Taiwan are set to increase this week. CPC Corp and Formosa Petrochemical Corp attributed the price hike to market factors, while international crude oil prices fell due to concerns over global oil demand amidst US-EU trade tensions. DBS Bank raised Taiwan's GDP growth forecast for 2023 to 4% from 3%, citing strong demand for AI-related exports and accelerated shipment activity. The Taiwan Institute of Economic Research also revised its forecast upwards to 3.5%, driven by robust export growth in the first half of the year. Delta Electronics Inc. reaffirmed its long-term global production plan, emphasizing its diversified global deployment and "produce at the market end" strategy. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC) reached a market value of over US$1 trillion for the first time, fueled by robust AI demand and a raised sales forecast.
