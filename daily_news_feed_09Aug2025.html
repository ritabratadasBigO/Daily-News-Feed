Published Date: Fri, 08 Aug 2025

A three-judge bench of the Supreme Court is currently reviewing the 2022 Vijay Madanlal judgment, which upheld several provisions of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) granting broad powers to the Enforcement Directorate (ED). Petitioners argue that the judgment undermines procedural safeguards for accused individuals, while the ED maintains that the review petition lacks merit and is an attempt to reargue a lost case. The court expressed concern over the ED's high volume of cases and low conviction rates, questioning the agency's investigative practices and urging it to improve transparency. The ED defended its actions, citing procedural delays often instigated by accused individuals and highlighting the challenges of investigating financial crimes, particularly those involving cryptocurrency. The court also discussed the constitutionality of amending the PMLA through a Money Bill, noting that this issue is currently pending before a larger bench. The hearing will continue to address preliminary issues and determine the maintainability of the review petition.
