Image not found.AU Small Finance Bank bags universal banking licence from RBI, first in 11 years - The Hindu

Published Date: Thu, 07 Aug 2025

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has granted in-principle approval to AU Small Finance Bank Ltd. (AUSFB) to transition from a small finance bank (SFB) to a universal bank. This marks the first time in 11 years that the RBI has issued a universal banking license, with the last approvals granted in 2014 to Bandhan Bank and IDFC Bank. AUSFB, established in 1996, received its SFB license in 2015 and has since grown to become the largest SFB in India, with a network spanning 21 states and 4 union territories. The transition to a universal bank is subject to AUSFB meeting specific criteria, including minimum paid-up capital, a satisfactory track record, and RBI due diligence. The RBI issued updated guidelines for SFBs to transition into universal banks in April 2024, providing greater clarity on the eligibility requirements. This development signifies a significant expansion of AUSFB's operations and its potential to offer a wider range of financial services.

Image not found.Money mules: Tackling payments fraud across the account lifecycle - Finextra Research

Published Date: Thu, 07 Aug 2025

Financial institutions are facing a growing threat from money mules, who facilitate fraud by diverting funds to newly opened accounts. This illicit activity costs banks billions annually, with losses exacerbated by the expenses associated with account opening and maintenance. Regulators are increasingly holding both sending and receiving banks accountable for money mule activity, as seen in the UK and the US. To combat this challenge, banks must adopt a comprehensive strategy that addresses money mule activity throughout the entire account lifecycle, from identity verification to closure. The early stages of an account are particularly vulnerable, as money mules exploit the lack of transaction history to move and cash out funds. A layered approach combining real-time monitoring, peer profiling, community analytics, and AML measures is crucial for effectively mitigating the systemic risk posed by money mules.
