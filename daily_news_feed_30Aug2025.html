Image not found.RMB gains to continue amid policy guidance - DBS

Published Date: Fri, 29 Aug 2025

DBS Bank India Limited has launched DBS Treasures, a premium banking and wealth management program tailored for high-net-worth individuals. The program offers personalized banking, wealth management, lifestyle, travel, and health privileges based on a tiered system based on Total Relationship Value (TRV). TRV is calculated by aggregating average quarterly balances across various accounts, including savings, term deposits, insurance, investments, and mortgage loans. DBS Treasures provides exclusive benefits such as signature lifestyle privileges, unlimited ATM withdrawals, and competitive forex markups on international transactions. The program also features a dedicated digital banking platform for managing finances and wealth. To qualify for DBS Treasures, individuals must maintain a minimum TRV of INR 30 Lacs.

Image not found.Japan’s Top 300 Brands Rise 6% in 2025, Led by Toyota for 10th Year - Branding in Asia

Published Date: Fri, 29 Aug 2025

Japan's top 300 brands saw a 6% year-on-year increase in brand value, reaching JPY 100 trillion in 2025, according to Brand Finance's Japan 300 2025 report. Automotive, telecoms, and insurance brands led the rankings, driven by strong market performance and investment, while food and tobacco brands declined. Toyota retained its position as Japan's most valuable brand for the tenth consecutive year, with a 20% increase in brand value to JPY 10 trillion, fueled by strong financial results and an upward revision of its annual profit forecast. NTT Group overtook Honda to take third place, driven by digital consolidation, global data center expansion, and momentum in AI and cloud services. NRI emerged as the fastest-growing brand, with its brand value doubling to JPY 100 billion, reflecting its leadership in AI, digital transformation, and sustainability. SECOM was named Japan's strongest brand, earning an AAA rating with a Brand Strength Index score of 90.1 out of 100.

Image not found.CredRight secures $10 million to accelerate MSME lending across India - Elets BFSI

Published Date: Fri, 29 Aug 2025

CredRight, an Indian MSME lender, secured $10 million in Series B funding led by Abler Nordic, marking the latter's first investment from Fund V. This investment addresses India's significant credit gap for MSMEs, estimated at $530 billion, with a focus on the underserved microenterprise sector. Existing investors, including the Michael & Susan Dell Foundation and Unleash Capital, also participated, demonstrating confidence in CredRight's growth potential. The funding will enable CredRight to expand its loan book, enhance its digital lending technology, and reach tier II to IV towns and rural areas. This funding round comes amidst increased regulatory scrutiny on unsecured lending in India, highlighting CredRight's commitment to sustainable and scalable credit delivery.
