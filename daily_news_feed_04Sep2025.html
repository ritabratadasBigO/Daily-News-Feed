Image not found.Big relief: Health and life insurance exempted from GST - Business Today

Published Date: Wed, 03 Sep 2025

The GST Council implemented significant tax reforms, exempting individual health and life insurance from GST, effective September 22nd. This move aims to reduce the financial burden on consumers and enhance healthcare accessibility. The council also abolished the 12% and 28% GST slabs, replacing them with a 40% bracket for luxury and sin goods. Daily essentials like hair oil, shampoo, and toothpaste will now be taxed at 5%, while medical items like thermometers and diagnostic kits are now GST-free. Industry experts applaud the decision, highlighting its positive impact on affordability and insurance penetration. They anticipate increased demand for insurance, particularly among first-time buyers, and a shift towards higher sum insured policies. The reforms are expected to drive financial inclusion and strengthen India's healthcare security.
