Published Date: Mon, 11 Nov 2024

DBS Group, Singapore's largest lender, is considering acquiring stakes in local banks in Malaysia, reported Reuters. This move aligns with DBS's strategy to establish a retail banking presence in Southeast Asia. The target is a 49% stake in Alliance Bank Malaysia, currently held by Temasek, valued at around $460 million. DBS is also looking to acquire Kuwait Finance House's Malaysian retail banking assets, valued at over $500 million. However, the discussions are in early stages, and any formal negotiations would require approval from Bank Negara Malaysia. DBS has made significant acquisitions in markets such as China, India, Indonesia, and Taiwan, but is the only Singaporean bank without a retail banking presence in Malaysia.

AI Drives New Challenges in Financial Fraud Detection - Global Banking And Finance Review

Published Date: Mon, 11 Nov 2024

NICE Actimize's General Manager, Yuval Marco, warns that AI has significantly enhanced the abilities of fraudsters, making their schemes more efficient and allowing them to evade detection for extended periods. Financial institutions are grappling with major fraud challenges, including authorized push payment (APP) fraud and account takeover controls. The 2024 Fraud Insights report by NICE Actimize highlighted a marked increase in authorized fraud volume, indicating that these scams successfully bypass traditional controls. Marco emphasizes that AI provides unparalleled detection and prevention capabilities, but financial institutions must enhance defenses at every stage, from account opening to comprehensive fraud monitoring. He advises financial institutions to take proactive measures, including enhancing onboarding and application fraud controls, developing specific detection models for money mule activities, and optimizing fraud investigation processes. Marco concludes that financial institutions must fully leverage AI across detection, strategy, and operations to achieve optimal fraud prevention.
