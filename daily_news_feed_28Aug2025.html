Image not found.New FICO Data Warns of Growing Fraud Risk in Credit Card Applications - CardRates.com

Published Date: Wed, 27 Aug 2025

A recent FICO survey revealed that up to one-third of Americans believe it's acceptable to lie on credit card applications, raising concerns about application fraud. This trend is particularly concerning as subprime borrowers, who are more likely to have difficulty obtaining traditional credit, are more inclined to seek new credit cards. While consumers may feel pressured to falsify information, doing so can lead to legal consequences, including fines and imprisonment. To combat this issue, financial institutions are implementing stricter identity checks during the application process, including facial recognition scans, to protect both themselves and consumers. Additionally, fintech companies like Casap are leveraging AI to analyze dispute histories and identify potential fraud, aiming to deter dishonest behavior and streamline the dispute resolution process. These measures highlight the growing importance of fraud prevention in the financial services industry, as both issuers and consumers strive to maintain trust and security in the digital age.

Image not found.Discover the Growth of India's Financial Services Industry - ibef.org

Published Date: Wed, 27 Aug 2025

India's financial services sector is experiencing rapid growth, driven by factors such as a large and expanding middle class, increasing digital adoption, and government initiatives. The asset under management (AUM) of the mutual fund industry has grown sixfold in the past decade, reaching Rs. trillion (US$ billion) in July 2024. With a focus on digitalization and innovation, India's financial services sector is poised to become a global powerhouse.

Image not found.IBDIC’s Blockchain Lending Tool for MSMEs Clears the Theme-Neutral Sandbox of RBI - MediaNama

Published Date: Wed, 27 Aug 2025

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) announced the successful exit of a blockchain-based deeptier financing solution from its Fifth Cohort of the Regulatory Sandbox. Developed by IBDIC Private Limited in partnership with leading Indian banks, the platform tokenizes supplier invoices, enabling lenders to provide financing against these tokens. This facilitates affordable credit for MSMEs within the supply chains of large, creditworthy companies. The RBI evaluated the product's viability during the Test Phase and determined it suitable for potential adoption by regulated entities, subject to compliance with existing regulations. The Fifth Cohort, the first to be "Theme Neutral," allowed applications from any area within the RBI's regulatory domain, showcasing the sandbox's commitment to fostering innovation across financial services. The RBI has further expanded the Regulatory Sandbox framework to accept applications on an "On Tap" basis, encouraging continuous innovation in areas like digital lending, blockchain, and sustainable finance.
