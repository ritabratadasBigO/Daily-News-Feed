Image not found.Less stigma, more support, but mental health insurance has a long way to go in India - The Federal

Published Date: Tue, 24 Jun 2025

The Indian mental health insurance landscape is undergoing a significant transformation. In 2021, the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) mandated coverage of mental illness, leading to a surge in claims, particularly for outpatient treatment like therapy and medication. Women are driving this change, with a 27% increase in purchasing policies with mental health riders, reflecting a growing awareness of mental wellbeing. While insurers like Bajaj Allianz and United India now cover inpatient and some outpatient mental health expenses, challenges remain. Cashless claims for outpatient care are still limited, and awareness about policy coverage remains low among patients. The stigma surrounding mental health also persists, hindering widespread access to care. Despite these hurdles, the industry is evolving, with insurers exploring innovative solutions like dedicated mental health packages and teleconsultations to address the growing need for accessible and comprehensive mental healthcare.

Image not found.Why does the stock market scare people? - Davy

Published Date: Mon, 23 Jun 2025

Gary Connolly, Head of Advisory and Execution Only at Davy, argues that while stock market investing offers significant wealth creation potential, it's often misunderstood due to a focus on short-term capital losses. He cites data showing that while individual company losses can be substantial, market declines are typically temporary, with historical US market performance averaging a 10% annual compound return after inflation. Connolly emphasizes that investors should focus on long-term growth and consider diversification through funds to mitigate risk. He compares the fear of short-term losses to an ice hockey team avoiding the "pulling the goalie" strategy, which, while risky, can lead to greater league points in the long run. Connolly encourages investors to overcome their fear of market drawdowns and recognize the potential of the stock market for maintaining purchasing power. He concludes by reminding investors that while past performance is not indicative of future results, a long-term perspective and a focus on risk management are crucial for successful investing.
