Image not found.WesBank and Toyota Financial Services Pay R30 Million to Settle Market Division Case with Competition Commission - Central News South Africa

Published Date: Thu, 28 Aug 2025

WesBank and Toyota Financial Services paid R30 million to settle a Competition Commission case alleging market division in Toyota vehicle financing. The settlement, reached without admitting liability, followed a complaint alleging that the companies allocated customers and suppliers, limiting competition. This allegedly involved restricting WesBank from offering finance to customers purchasing vehicles at authorized Toyota dealerships, funneling them to a joint venture. As part of the settlement, these restrictions have been removed, giving customers more financing choices. The Competition Tribunal approved the settlement in August 2025, concluding the proceedings. This resolution ensures increased competition and more options for consumers seeking vehicle financing from authorized Toyota dealerships.

Image not found.Discover the Growth of India's Financial Services Industry - ibef.org

Published Date: Thu, 28 Aug 2025

India's financial services sector is experiencing rapid growth, driven by factors such as a large and expanding middle class, increasing digital adoption, and government initiatives. The asset under management (AUM) of the mutual fund industry has grown sixfold in the last decade, reaching Rs. trillion (US$ billion) in July 2024. The insurance sector is also expanding, with investment corpus projected to reach US$ 1 trillion by 2025, driven by rising penetration in rural areas and increased participation from high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs). Furthermore, initiatives like the Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana have significantly boosted financial inclusion. With a supportive regulatory environment and a growing economy, India's financial services sector is poised for continued expansion and global prominence.

Image not found.Indian bank margins likely to improve in fiscal H2 after slow Q1: S&P Global - BusinessLine

Published Date: Thu, 28 Aug 2025

Indian banks experienced mixed results in the first quarter of FY26, with net income declining for four of the top six lenders. This decline was partly attributed to the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) interest rate cuts, which impacted net interest margins (NIMs). SBI and ICICI Bank bucked the trend, reporting strong earnings growth, while other major banks like Bank of Baroda, Punjab National Bank, HDFC Bank, and Axis Bank saw net income decrease. NIMs also faced pressure across the board, with SBI and HDFC Bank reporting notable declines. The RBI's recent decision to reduce the cash reserve ratio and lower its benchmark lending rate aims to inject liquidity into the banking system and support economic growth. Despite some concerns about asset quality, analysts do not anticipate a significant rise in nonperforming loans.

Fonterra has dropped to seventh place in Rabobank's Global Dairy Top 20, after selling its consumer business, Mainland, to Lactalis earlier this year.
